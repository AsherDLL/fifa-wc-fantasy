Research

How the models earned their places. This page reconstructs the evolution of the prediction stack from the evidence on disk, generation by generation, including the ideas that were measured and rejected. Every number can be regenerated with the commands at the end; the whitepaper remains the full scholarly account and each section is linked where it applies.

Reading the evidence. Two kinds of numbers appear below and they must not be mixed. Held-out and walk-forward tables never let a model see its test labels and are the trustworthy basis for decisions. The realized backtest scores squads on rounds the WC-retrained GBM later trained on, so gbm and ensemble rows there are upper bounds, useful for narrative and unfair for comparison.
1

Model generations

  1. 2026-06-07 Heuristic v1

    Price-anchored baseline ships so the optimizer has predictions before a ball is kicked.
  2. 2026-06-07 GBM v1 and v2

    First learned model overfits a single EPL season; the same day, three seasons and tuned hyperparameters take midfield and forward on held-out EPL.
  3. 2026-06-07 Poisson backend

    A structural xG model joins as the interpretable cross-check and the goalkeeper specialist.
  4. 2026-06-28 GK formula v2

    The save bonus becomes opponent-xG scaled at an empirically calibrated 0.50, after the theoretical 1.13 measured worse.
  5. 2026-06-28 Monte Carlo and the backtest harness

    A distribution-producing simulator arrives with the cross-model squad backtest that scores every backend on realized rounds.
  6. 2026-06-28 Two negative results recorded

    Heuristic v2 loses every round to v1, and the Benter market combiner makes every backend worse; both are documented and shelved.
  7. 2026-07-08 GBM v3form and the ensemble

    A leak-free trailing-form feature plus retraining on completed World Cup rounds fixes the form-blindness defect; per-position routing becomes the production backend.

Superseded generations

GenerationStatusIntroducedWhy it was replaced
GBM v1 superseded 2026-06-07 Single EPL season (2024-25), LightGBM defaults with 31 leaves and 400 trees. It overfit league idiosyncrasies, famously clustering Scandinavian defenders and proposing Chris Wood as vice-captain of a World Cup squad. Superseded the same day it was benchmarked.
GBM v2 superseded 2026-06-07 Three EPL seasons (34,221 rows) and tuned hyperparameters, 15 leaves and 200 trees. It won midfielders and forwards on the held-out EPL benchmark and shipped as the tournament starter, but it was form-blind: five features, all price and strength, none about how the player had actually been scoring. That defect is what v3form fixed.
Heuristic v2 rejected 2026-06-28 Six stacked bonuses on top of the v1 formula, including a 0.40 realized-form anchor. It lost to plain v1 in every backtested round, cumulative 190 against 211, a textbook case of adding components faster than they can be validated. Kept in the tree for reproducibility; never routed.

Held-out validation and routing

The canonical benchmark holds out EPL 2024-25 gameweeks 30 to 38. No single backend wins every position there. Poisson takes goalkeepers, the heuristic takes defenders, the GBM takes midfielders and forwards, and the production ensemble is precisely that routing table applied per position.

4

Walk-forward validation on World Cup rounds

The EPL benchmark cannot answer whether a change survives the shift to international football. The walk-forward design can. For each completed round, the GBM trains strictly on earlier data and predicts that round, so form features and WC retraining are tested leak-free. The shipped configuration, WC rows plus the trailing-form feature, wins pooled RMSE, and config D shows why the minutes features were rejected.

6

The goalkeeper formula, theory against measurement

The save bonus began as a flat constant, was re-derived from first principles at 1.13 per unit of opponent xG, and shipped at the empirically calibrated 0.50 after the sweep showed the theoretical value overestimates. Not all opponent xG becomes shots on target, and the median keeper does not save at the elite rate. The episode set the project rule that derivations propose and measurements decide.

Negative results

Rejected ideas are results. Each row below was built, measured under the same gates as everything that shipped, and turned down on the numbers. Recording them keeps the same mistake from being made twice and keeps the shipped stack honest about why it looks the way it does.

IdeaTestedWhat the measurement saidEvidence
Heuristic v2 component stack
Six intuition-driven bonuses added at once to the heuristic. Every backtested round preferred the unmodified v1. The lesson written into the whitepaper is procedural, one component at a time, each behind a held-out gate.		 2026-06-28 Cumulative realized points 190 vs 211 for unmodified v1 backtest_summary.json
team_elo_diff as a GBM feature
Adding the Elo gap as a sixth GBM feature was a wash on held-out EPL and carried distribution-shift risk, since international Elo gaps dwarf club gaps. The signal is consumed instead by the heuristic and Poisson backends, where it is used directly.		 2026-07-08 EPL held-out RMSE: GK -0.018, MID -0.019, DEF +0.033, FWD +0.015 gbm.py
GK save bonus, theoretical 1.13 multiplier
The save-bonus multiplier derived from first principles (shots per xG times save rate over saves per bonus point) overestimated reality. Not all opponent xG becomes on-target shots and the median keeper does not save at the elite rate. The empirical sweep landed on 0.50, which shipped as GK formula v2.		 2026-06-28 Empirical sweep minimum at 0.50, not the derived 1.13 gk_formula_ab_2026-06-29.json gk_formula_ab.py
start_rate_lag and team_gc_form as GBM features
Config D of the walk-forward validation added participation and team defensive form to the feature set. Both regressed the leak-free RMSE at every position; the participation proxy is too noisy over four rounds and defensive form is collinear with the strength signals. Each signal is still used where it helps, in the Poisson clean-sheet term and the rotation-risk flag.		 2026-07-08 Pooled walk-forward RMSE 3.281 to 3.307 when added wc_forward_validation.json wc_forward_validation.py
Benter combiner with Polymarket prices
Combining model predictions with prediction-market win probabilities, after Benter's racetrack architecture, made every backend worse in every round. Tournament-winner contracts carry no per-player, per-match information the models lack. Market data stays on the intelligence page as context and deliberately does not feed the models.		 2026-06-28 delta RMSE positive for all backend-round pairs (combined worse) with_vs_without_market.json with_vs_without_market.py
GK defensive-form adjustment
Scaling goalkeeper predictions by the team's recent goals conceded looked sensible and measured worse. Four rounds of conceded goals is mostly schedule, not skill, and the strength signals already carry the opponent.		 2026-07-08 Walk-forward GK RMSE regressed when applied wc_forward_validation.py
